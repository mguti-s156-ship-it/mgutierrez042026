# Life In Feudal Europe
- most medieval europeans lived in small villages 
    - centered on a manor, church, and peasant homes

    feudal society:
    - lords- owned land
    - knights- provided military service
    - serfs- farmed

    life for serfs was harsh:
    - high infant mortality
    - short life expectancy
    - poor sanitation, and disease
- life revolved around agriculture, the seasons, and the church
- farming yields often low-
    - famine was common
    - weather, disease, pests
- The Catholic Church:
    - only organized instution in medieval europe 
    - own vast lands
    - gained wealth through tithes and rents 
- 1054: The Great Schism
    - church split
    - estern church- constantinople
    - western church- rome
